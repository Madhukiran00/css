Alexander Graham Bell

Alexander Graham Bell was a Scottish-born inventor, scientist, and engineer best known for inventing the telephone in 1876. He was born on March 3, 1847, in Edinburgh, Scotland. Bell had a deep interest in sound and speech, influenced by his work with the deaf. He founded the Bell Telephone Company in 1877. Besides the telephone, he also worked on innovations in aeronautics and hydrofoils. Bell was a dedicated teacher and helped in the development of communication methods for the hearing impaired. He received many honors for his contributions to science and technology. Alexander Graham Bell passed away on August 2, 1922, in Nova Scotia, Canada.
